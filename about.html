I'm an academic researcher and programmer who brings his interpretive and technical know-how to compelling human problems.

I'm finishing up my PhD at the University of Chicago's Committee on Social Thought, where I wrote a dissertation about individualism in modern Western culture.

Besides my recent dissertation work, I have an active and growing portfolio of skills and projects related to web tech, data analytics, and working with digital content. Before getting my PhD, I worked at the New America Foundation, where I designed and helped implement stimulus.org (now archived), a nationally-recognized online database for journalists and policymakers to help track federal government actions related to the 2009 financial crisis. I was also previously an analyst doing best-practices research on medical technology trends for the Advisory Board Company.

I view my research and technical interests as complementary skill sets. Both require insatiable curiosity and a good instinct for staying on top of new trends. As a writer, my work has often focused on how science and technology are transforming everyday life. As a tech geek, I have spent significant time and effort building coding skills that can be applied to real-world problems! At the conclusion of my Ph.D., I intend to move into the private sector and pursue a career in technology full time.

See the Projects page and my LinkedIN account for more details on my professional interests.

Outside of work, I am an accomplished amateur runner who has competed in distances from 5k to ultra-marathon. I'm also a dedicated backpacker and outdoorsman with dozens of trips logged across North America in all four seasons, and an avid fly-fisher and fly-tyer.
